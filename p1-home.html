Let me introduce myself:

I am currently studying at University College Dublin in the Master's Computer Science Conversion degree. Lots of change but also lots of new knowledge.

What are my goals you might ask?

I want to contribute to innovating the technology of tomorrow, especially in the arts sector. The innovative force of art is often underestimated. Art continuously innovates itself, yet many people hold onto this image of it as oil paintings on white museum walls. Lately, this preconceived notion is fading fast: Christie's auction house sold Beeple's NFT artwork for $69m, UNESCO invested significantly in digitisation of cultural heritage, and virtual exhibitions became the centre of curatorial activities, like the Tate Modern's Andy Warhol video tour. These emerging intersectional pathways of art and computer science are a vast and promising career direction.
